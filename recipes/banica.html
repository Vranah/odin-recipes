Banica

Description:

Banitsa (Bulgarian: баница), also transliterated as banica and banitza, is a traditional pastry made in Bulgaria. It is also made in Budjak, where it is known as milina by Ukrainian Bulgarians; North Macedonia; and southeastern Serbia. In southeastern Serbia, it may also be known as gibanica.

Ingridients:

  • Dough sheets
  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Bulgarian yogurt
  • Bulgarian white chees
  • Vegtable oil

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C, fan 180°C, gas 4. Grease a 20cm round baking dish with butter
  2. In a mixing bowl, lightly whisk together the eggs, yogurt and oil
  3. Lay one sheet of pastry out on your work surface and drizzle over some of the egg and yogurt mixture
  4. Scatter over some bulgarian white chees and then roll up the pastry from one of the short sides, into a cigar shape
  5. Place in the prepared baking dish, following the edge
  6. Repeat with the remaining sheets, spiralling the rolls until the dish is full
  7. Spoon any remaining egg and yogurt mixture into any gaps in the coil and then dot the top with butter.
  8. Bake for 45 minutes, or until risen and golden brown.
  9. Remove from the oven, cover with a clean cloth and leave to cool until warm
